Warm-up Exercises

STRENGTH EXERCISE

Warm-up exercises are vital preparatory activities before physical workouts. Lasting 5-15 minutes, they gradually increase heart rate, circulation, and body temperature. Common activities include light jogging, dynamic stretching, and joint rotations. Benefits include injury prevention, improved performance, mental readiness, and enhanced post-workout recovery. They optimize physical readiness and workout effectiveness.

Strength Training Exercises

WEIGHT LIFTING

Strength training exercises are workouts aimed at building muscle mass, strength, and endurance. They involve resistance against muscles through weights, resistance bands, or bodyweight. Common exercises include squats, deadlifts, and bench presses. Benefits include increased muscle mass, improved bone density, enhanced metabolism, and overall physical performance.

Cardio Exercises

CARDIO EXERCISE

Cardio exercises, short for cardiovascular exercises, are activities that elevate heart rate and improve cardiovascular health. Examples include running, cycling, swimming, and jumping rope. These exercises primarily target the heart and lungs, improving endurance, circulation, and overall fitness. Benefits include weight loss, improved heart health, increased energy levels, and stress reduction.

Flexibility Exercises

PLANK

Flexibility exercises focus on improving range of motion and muscle elasticity. They include stretching exercises that target major muscle groups and joints, such as hamstring stretches, shoulder stretches, and spinal twists. Benefits include reduced risk of injury, improved posture, enhanced athletic performance, and increased overall mobility and flexibility.
